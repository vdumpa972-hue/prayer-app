Yes — that is exactly the right next move.

What you are describing is a real owner dashboard, and it should be a separate page or small group of pages under your owner app. That is much better than mixing owner controls into the master app.

I would keep the structure like this. prayer-master stays the product your customers use. prayer-follower stays the simple viewer. prayer-app becomes your internal control center as owner.

For the owner dashboard, I would split it into a few simple sections. First, a Clients section where you can see each account, role, subscription type, status, member since, last login if you later track it, and whether the account is active or blocked. From there you should be able to add a client, edit their profile, change their plan, suspend them, or delete them.

Second, a Billing section. This should show who is paid, unpaid, late, canceled, trial, and expiring soon. It should also show alerts like overdue payments, failed renewals, and subscriptions ending soon. This is where owner-only payment actions should live.

Third, a Business overview section. This should show simple numbers at the top: total customers, active paid customers, monthly recurring revenue, yearly revenue estimate, overdue accounts, and new customers this month. That gives you a quick pulse of the company.

Fourth, a Customer activity section. Later, this can show session counts, most active customers, who has not used the app lately, and maybe which prayer libraries they use most. That helps with support and sales decisions.

So yes, I strongly agree with your idea. In fact, I would say this is more important than payments UI right now, because once you have an owner dashboard, payments and subscriptions have a proper place to live.

A good first version would include only these owner pages: Dashboard, Clients, Billing, and maybe Settings. That is enough to start. No need to overbuild yet.

I would also add role protections very clearly. Owner can see everything. Admin, if you add one later, can maybe see clients and help with support but not full financial control. User cannot access this dashboard at all.

For the client table, I would start with these fields: full name, email, role, plan, status, member since, phone, and notes. Then later add payment status and last activity.

For the dashboard summary cards, I would start with: total users, paid users, overdue users, and estimated monthly revenue. That gives immediate value without a lot of complexity.

So my recommendation is: yes, create a new owner page for this. Do not put it inside prayer-master. Put it in prayer-app, and make prayer-app owner-only.

The best next coding step is to build a simple owner dashboard shell with navigation and placeholder data first, then wire Firebase data into it.

Yes — that fits very well, but I would do it in two layers so we do not tangle login and billing too early.

For prayer-master, the login screen can absolutely have extra entry choices, but I would name them a little more clearly. Keep the normal Login for existing customers. Then add Start 30-Day Trial for new users. Then add Join / Choose Plan or Subscribe for people who are ready to pay. That makes sense.

The important design point is this: the login page itself should not directly become a huge payment form. It should act as the gateway. So the flow should be:

Existing user goes to login.
New user who wants to try goes to trial signup.
New user who wants to pay goes to plan selection, then payment, then account creation or activation.

That is cleaner, easier to maintain, and much safer.

So I would build the next step like this.

First, create an owner dashboard in prayer-app that is owner-only. It should have a simple home screen with company stats and a clients table. From there you can view users, edit plans, and later handle billing alerts.

Second, enhance prayer-master /auth so it has three clear paths:
Login, Start 30-Day Trial, and Join / Subscribe.

Third, for now, we can make the Trial and Join flows as working product pages even before full payment integration is finished. For example, trial can create a user with plan trial and a trial end date. Join can take the user to a plan selection page first. Later we connect Stripe.

That means the account model should start expanding a bit. Besides role, we should add fields like subscriptionType, subscriptionStatus, trialStart, trialEnd, and memberSince. That will help both the owner dashboard and the future payment system.

I strongly recommend this sequence:
first owner dashboard shell, then trial/join flow, then Stripe hookup. That keeps things stable.

So yes, I agree with your idea, but I would not put raw payment entry fields directly inside the current login card yet. I would make the login card show the three buttons and route people to the proper next page.

A good first version would be:
/auth with Login, Start 30-Day Trial, and Join / Subscribe buttons.
/trial page for creating a trial account.
/plans page for choosing subscription type.
/owner or root prayer-app dashboard for your management view.

That is the right business direction.
